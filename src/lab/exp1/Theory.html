Preparation of lattice and crystal models

Crystal

A 3D translationally periodic arrangement of atoms in a space is called crystal. It is a physical object for which we can calculate weight, density etc.

Lattice

A 3D translationally periodic arrangement of points in a space is called lattice. It is a geometrical concept for which we can calculate only geometric properties.

Motif/Basis

It is the linking bridge between crystal and lattice. An atom or group of atoms associated with each lattice point is called motif.

Relation between crystal,lattice and motif

Crystal = Lattice + Motif
• Lattice tells us how to repeat
• Motif tells us what to repeat





Unit cell

A region of space which can generate the entire lattice(or crystal) by repetition through lattice translations.It is parallellogram in 20, parallelopiped in 30 with corners at lattice points. A given lattice can have many unit cells.

A unit cell is classified into two types

1. Primitive : Lattice points are present only at the corners of unit cell.



2. Non-primitive : Lattice points at corners as well as other places in the unit cell. These can be divided into following types-
• Body Centered- In this lattice points are found on the cell corners with one additional lattice point at the center of the cell.



• Face Centered- In this lattice points are found on the cell corners with one additional lattice point at the center of each face of the cell.



• Base Centered- In this lattice points are found on the cell corners with one additional lattice point at the center of each face of one pair of parallel faces of the cell. It is also called end centered.





Lattice parameters

The length of three edges of unit cell and the three internal angles between them are called lattice parameters

CLASSIFICATION OF LATTICES 

Lattices are classified based on their symmetry. There are seven crystal systems and 14 bravias lattices.


Miller Indices

• Miller indices are used to specify directions and planes.
• These directions and planes could be in lattices or in crystals.
• The number of indices will match with the dimension of the lattice or the crystal.
• E.g. in 1D there will be 1 index and 2D there will be two indices etc.

Notation

• (h,k,l) represents a point – note the exclusive use of commas
• Negative numbers/directions are denoted with a bar on top of the number
• [hkl] represents a direction
• <hkl> represents a family of directions
• (hkl) represents a plane
• {hkl} represents a family of planes
